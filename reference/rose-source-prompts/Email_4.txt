TREATMENT PLAN PROMPT INSTRUCTIONS

Create a Treatment Plan based on the transcript provided.

This treatment plan form is separate from the BPS and Diagnostic/Clinical Impressions form. The output must be copy/paste ready for the Treatment Plan form.

ABSOLUTE FORMATTING RULES

Every section header must appear alone on its own line.

The content for that section must begin after exactly one blank line.

Never place content on the same line as a header.

Use single line breaks only unless explicitly instructed otherwise.

Do not use em dashes.

Use professional clinical language.

Write in third person.

Always use the client’s first name throughout the entire treatment plan.

Do not use “the client” unless it appears in the Safety Planning language and is clinically necessary.

Do not use bullet points.

Do not use paragraph formatting for Objectives or Therapeutic Interventions.

Keep everything copy/paste ready for an EHR.

ASSESSMENT DATE RULE

Assessment Date must always be an exact copy of the Date of Service Plan.

Do not calculate the Assessment Date separately.

Do not leave Assessment Date blank.

Example:

Date of Service Plan:
5/14/2026

Assessment Date:
5/14/2026

STRENGTHS SECTION RULES

Strengths must be written in paragraph form.

Strengths should usually be 4 to 6 sentences.

Strengths should be individualized based on the transcript and clinical information.

Strengths may include:

insight into substance use history
motivation for recovery
active engagement in treatment
participation in structured recovery services
supportive relationships
family or sober supports
medication adherence
employment history
education history
interest in hobbies
spirituality
willingness to discuss needs
openness to treatment
self-awareness
protective factors

Do not write generic strengths.

Do not overstate strengths that are not supported by the transcript.

RISK FACTORS SECTION RULES

Risk Factors must be written in paragraph form.

Risk Factors should usually be 5 to 8 sentences.

Risk Factors should be individualized and clinically meaningful.

Risk Factors should support ongoing medical necessity when the client has a Substance Use Disorder and is appropriate for ongoing services.

Risk Factors may include:

substance use history
severity of use
relapse history
recent use
withdrawal symptoms
detox history
inpatient or residential treatment history
legal involvement
probation or parole
unemployment
financial instability
housing instability
limited sober supports
medical concerns
mental health symptoms
medication nonadherence
environmental relapse risk
cravings
transportation barriers
history of suicidal ideation
history of self-harm
history of violence
current safety concerns

Do not invent risk factors.

Do not minimize clinically relevant risk.

OBJECTIVE AND INTERVENTION NUMBERED FORMAT RULES

Each problem must contain exactly:

1 Goal
3 Objectives
3 Therapeutic Interventions

Do not generate fewer than 3 objectives.

Do not generate more than 3 objectives.

Do not generate fewer than 3 therapeutic interventions.

Do not generate more than 3 therapeutic interventions.

Objectives must always be formatted as a numbered list using this exact format:

1. [One complete sentence.]
2. [One complete sentence.]
3. [One complete sentence.]

Each objective must be one complete sentence.

Do not combine multiple sentences under one number.

Do not use bullet points.

Do not use paragraph formatting for objectives.

Therapeutic Interventions must always be formatted as a numbered list using this exact format:

1. [One complete sentence.]
2. [One complete sentence.]
3. [One complete sentence.]

Each therapeutic intervention must be one complete sentence.

Do not combine multiple sentences under one number.

Do not use bullet points.

Do not use paragraph formatting for interventions.

Therapeutic Interventions should usually begin with “Clinician will…”

GENERAL TREATMENT PLAN RULES

Write SMART objectives as direct clinical statements.

Objectives should be measurable, behavioral, and clinically realistic.

Goals must be one sentence only.

Goals must be broad and rehabilitation-focused.

Goals must support medical necessity.

Goals must be broad enough to support Medicaid/QBHA linkage and multiple clinical interventions.

Goals must not be overly narrow or task-specific.

Problem Statements must be one concise clinical paragraph.

Problem Statements should usually be 1 to 3 sentences.

Problem Statements must focus on ongoing treatment need and medical necessity.

Problem Statements must reflect relapse risk, functional impairment, emotional regulation needs, life skills needs, recovery support needs, or referral needs depending on the case.

Do not frame Problem Statements as short-term stabilization only unless detox or higher level of care is specifically indicated.

REVIEW/COMMENTS RULE

Review/Comments should be left blank.

Do not auto-populate Review/Comments unless specifically instructed.

STANDARD SUD TREATMENT PLAN RULE

When the client meets criteria for a Substance Use Disorder and is appropriate for ongoing outpatient or intensive outpatient services, the treatment plan must reflect:

ongoing clinical services
continued need for structured support
active participation in programming
medical necessity for treatment
relapse prevention needs
recovery support needs
life skills development
emotional regulation needs

If information in the transcript appears inconsistent, default to continued treatment need when the client has an SUD and is appropriate for ongoing care.

FORBIDDEN LANGUAGE FOR STANDARD SUD TREATMENT PLANS

For standard ongoing SUD treatment plans, do not use:

30-day
transition
discharge
continuity of care period
stabilization period

Do not reference leaving services in standard ongoing SUD treatment plans.

ROLE CLARIFICATION

The clinician’s role is:

assessment
diagnosis
treatment planning
clinical recommendations
supporting engagement
encouraging participation
recommending resources
coordinating care when clinically appropriate

The clinician is not responsible for:

housing placement
housing removal
enforcing employment timelines
enforcing housing timelines
ensuring client follow-through
guaranteeing outcomes
securing employment
securing housing

Sober living housing is independent of clinical services and determined by housing staff.

Sober living may be referenced as a recovery environment or support when appropriate, but it should not be framed as a clinical level of care.

Peer coaches may assist with connecting clients to resources.

The client is responsible for engaging in recommended services.

MEDICAID AND QBHA ALIGNMENT REQUIREMENT

All goals must be written broadly enough to support linkage to QBHA-delivered services and Colorado Medicaid billing codes, including:

H2014
S9454
97535
97537

Goals must:

support medical necessity
allow linkage across multiple group topics
reflect rehabilitative and skills-based programming
avoid narrow single-issue phrasing
support DAP note linkage
support audit defensibility

STANDARD REQUIRED THREE PROBLEM DOMAINS FOR SUD CLIENTS

When the client has a Substance Use Disorder and is appropriate for outpatient or intensive outpatient services, use three treatment problems:

PROBLEM #1:
Recovery

PROBLEM #2:
Life Skills

PROBLEM #3:
Emotional Regulation

PROBLEM #1 RECOVERY DOMAIN

Problem #1 should focus on recovery stability, relapse prevention, treatment engagement, and ongoing recovery support.

Problem Statement should describe the client’s ongoing recovery needs, relapse risk, substance use history, prior treatment episodes, cravings, triggers, or need for structured clinical support.

Goal should focus on maintaining engagement in recovery services, relapse prevention, and overall recovery stability.

Objectives should include measurable recovery-related actions, such as:

attending recovery-oriented groups
developing a relapse prevention plan
identifying triggers and coping strategies
participating in individual counseling
engaging in medication-assisted treatment when applicable
using sober supports
maintaining communication with providers
following through with recovery recommendations

Therapeutic Interventions should include clinician actions, such as:

facilitating recovery skills groups
providing relapse prevention counseling
assisting with development or review of relapse prevention plans
coordinating with MAT providers when applicable
supporting engagement in sober supports
providing psychoeducation on relapse prevention and recovery maintenance

PROBLEM #2 LIFE SKILLS DOMAIN

Problem #2 should focus on independent functioning, daily living skills, vocational skills, financial stability, resource navigation, and rehabilitation.

Problem Statement should describe functional impairments, barriers to independent living, unemployment, financial instability, housing-related needs, transportation issues, lack of community resources, or other practical barriers that impact recovery stability.

Goal should focus on improving independent living skills, managing daily responsibilities, increasing stability, and strengthening rehabilitation-related functioning.

Objectives should include measurable life skills actions, such as:

attending life skills or vocational rehabilitation groups
developing a budget
identifying community resources
following through with appointments
working on employment-readiness skills
improving daily routine
using peer coaching or case management supports
addressing transportation or documentation needs

Therapeutic Interventions should include clinician actions, such as:

providing psychoeducation on budgeting and daily living skills
facilitating life skills groups
supporting resource identification
coordinating with peer coaches or case management when appropriate
encouraging use of community resources
assisting with problem-solving barriers to independent functioning

PROBLEM #3 EMOTIONAL REGULATION DOMAIN

Problem #3 should focus on coping skills, distress tolerance, emotional regulation, impulse control, stress management, and mental health stability.

Problem Statement should describe emotional dysregulation, stress, trauma-related symptoms, anxiety, depression, grief, impulsivity, anger, coping deficits, or other emotional/behavioral needs that may impact recovery and functioning.

Goal should focus on improving the client’s ability to manage stress, regulate emotions, use adaptive coping strategies, and support mental health stability.

Objectives should include measurable emotional regulation actions, such as:

attending group or individual sessions focused on emotional regulation
identifying coping strategies
practicing distress tolerance skills
using mindfulness or grounding techniques
reporting reduced emotional distress
identifying emotional triggers
developing a coping plan
using support systems when distressed

Therapeutic Interventions should include clinician actions, such as:

facilitating skills-based groups
providing counseling to reinforce coping skills
providing psychoeducation on emotional regulation and distress tolerance
monitoring emotional status
supporting crisis planning when clinically indicated
referring to mental health services when appropriate

STANDARD DATE RULES FOR OUTPATIENT OR IOP SUD CLIENTS

For clients appropriate for outpatient, intensive outpatient, or ongoing SUD treatment:

Target Date:
90 days

Completion Date:
approximately 3 months from the Assessment Date

Completion Date must be month and year only.

Do not include exact dates for standard 90-day outpatient or IOP treatment plans.

Example:

Assessment Date:
5/14/2026

Target Date:
90 days

Completion Date:
August 2026

Apply this to all three standard SUD treatment problems.

DETOX OVERRIDE RULE

If the clinician indicates the client needs detox first, detox must become PROBLEM #1.

Do not place standard Recovery, Life Skills, or Emotional Regulation as Problem #1 when detox is required first.

PROBLEM #1 must be detox-focused.

The detox problem should reflect:

active substance use
withdrawal risk
medical necessity
need for medical monitoring
detox engagement
withdrawal stabilization
preparation for continued treatment after detox

Detox Objectives should be detox-specific and may include:

admitting to detox
participating in detox services
completing withdrawal monitoring
following medical recommendations
engaging in discharge planning from detox
agreeing to return to outpatient or IOP services after detox when clinically appropriate

Detox Therapeutic Interventions should be detox-specific and may include:

Clinician will provide detox referral information.
Clinician will coordinate with detox or treatment providers when appropriate.
Clinician will encourage client to follow medical recommendations.
Clinician will support planning for continued treatment after detox completion.
Clinician will document detox recommendation and follow-up needs.

Dates for Detox Problem:

Target Date:
7–10 days

Completion Date:
exact projected date range 7 to 10 days from the Assessment Date

Example:

Assessment Date:
May 10, 2026

Target Date:
7–10 days

Completion Date:
May 17–20, 2026

After detox, PROBLEM #2 and PROBLEM #3 should resume standard ongoing treatment planning based on the client’s needs.

Problem #2 and Problem #3 may include language such as “following return from detox” or “after detox completion” when clinically appropriate.

For PROBLEM #2 and PROBLEM #3 after detox:

Target Date:
90 days

Completion Date:
month and year approximately 3 months from the Assessment Date

HIGHER LEVEL OF CARE RULE

If the client requires residential treatment, inpatient treatment, or another higher level of care, the treatment plan must reflect that level of need.

Do not force standard outpatient goals onto higher-level-of-care cases.

Problem statements, goals, objectives, and interventions should reflect:

need for higher level of care
admission support
treatment engagement
stabilization needs
coordination of care
medical necessity
preparation for continued treatment
safety or monitoring needs when applicable

Target Date:
TBD

Completion Date:
TBD

Use TBD for each problem where the timeframe depends on admission, stabilization, or the receiving provider’s recommendations.

NO SUBSTANCE USE DISORDER / REFERRAL-OUT RULE

If the client does not have a Substance Use Disorder, does not meet criteria for a Substance Use Disorder, or lacks a substance use history, do not write a standard SUD treatment plan.

The treatment plan must shift to a referral-out framework.

Do not include:

SUD goals
relapse prevention goals
recovery programming goals
peer recovery goals
MAT goals
SUD group objectives
substance use treatment objectives
IOP participation objectives
SUD peer coaching objectives

Clearly state in the relevant problem statements that the client requires referral out due to lack of substance use disorder history and/or failure to meet SUD criteria.

For no-SUD cases, the plan should focus on connection to clinically appropriate non-SUD services, such as:

outpatient mental health therapy
psychiatry
primary care
medical care
housing resources
case management
transportation support
grief support
community support
benefits assistance
vocational resources
other clinically indicated community resources

For no-SUD cases, each problem must be referral/resource focused and should not imply the client is appropriate for ongoing SUD services.

No-SUD Target Date:
30 days

No-SUD Completion Date:
approximately 30 days from the Assessment Date

Completion Date must be month and year only.

Example:

Assessment Date:
May 18, 2026

Target Date:
30 days

Completion Date:
June 2026

Apply the 30-day target date and next-month completion date to all problems when the client is being referred out due to no Substance Use Disorder.

SAFETY PLANNING RULE

Safety Planning must override any pre-populated form language.

Do not preserve default form text.

Use the client-specific safety planning language generated from the transcript and Bastion’s current safety-planning logic.

Safety Planning must reflect the client’s current presentation.

If no current suicidal or homicidal ideation is reported, use language similar to:

Client reports no current suicidal or homicidal ideation. The client was offered the 24/7 emergency services line for Colorado Crisis Services (844-493-8255) and the clinic’s local emergent services number.

If suicidal ideation, homicidal ideation, self-harm risk, violence risk, or elevated safety concerns are present, generate individualized safety-planning language based on the transcript and clinical presentation.

Safety Planning should include crisis resources and clinically appropriate recommendations.

NEXT REVIEW RULE

Next review on or before:
180 days

Use 180 days unless specifically instructed otherwise.
