Reliatrax BPS Automation Specification
Purpose and Workflow Overview
This document is intended to guide automation of the Reliatrax BPS form using structured output from Bastion GPT. The current workflow is:

Clinician completes the BPS interview verbally with the client.

Clinician does not ask every single question on the Reliatrax form.

Bastion GPT generates structured clinical responses from the interview.

Automation should transfer Bastion GPT output into the correct Reliatrax fields.

Some fields should be populated from direct client answers.

Some fields should use default answers every time unless the client specifically states otherwise.

Some fields require inference from the broader interview rather than direct copying.

Some fields are intentionally repurposed because the Reliatrax form does not provide a separate text box for the information the clinician needs documented.

If a field does not apply or there is no information to fill in, use n/a unless this document specifies another response such as Information not provided, Unknown, or leaving a checkbox blank.

Do not invent clinical information. When information is missing and the rule does not specify a default, use the appropriate missing-information phrase.

The overall goal is to reduce manual copy/paste from Bastion GPT into Reliatrax while keeping the form clinically accurate, consistent, and formatted the way the clinician currently completes it manually.

Global Rules
Client Naming and Clinical Voice
Use the client's first name in narrative responses when clinically appropriate.

Prefer full-sentence clinical documentation.

Use phrases such as:

[Client first name] reports...

[Client first name] denies...

[Client first name] states...

[Client first name] cannot recall...

For client-reported legal, medical, mental health, substance use, and family history information, frame it as client report rather than objective fact.

Avoid overly casual phrasing.

Drug/substance terminology rule: Use formal medical terminology for all drugs and substance names. Normalize slang or street names to medical terms, for example methamphetamine instead of meth or crystal meth, fentanyl instead of fent, and Opioids (Percocet) instead of Percs.

Avoid em dashes.

Keep responses concise but clinically complete.

Missing Information Rules
Use these rules unless a field-specific rule says otherwise:

If a field does not apply, enter n/a.

If the client was asked but does not know or cannot recall, use a full sentence such as:

[Client first name] reports they do not know.

[Client first name] cannot recall this information.

If the client was not asked and there is no default/inference rule, use Information not provided.

Do not leave required text fields blank unless this document explicitly says to leave the field blank.

For checkbox fields, only select a checkbox if the logic in this document indicates which option should be selected.

Direct Ask vs Default vs Inference
There are four types of fields:

Direct Ask / Direct Entry: The clinician asks the client directly. Use the client’s response.

Default Logic: The clinician does not routinely ask the question and wants the same default response unless the client clearly says something different.

Inference Logic: The system should infer the most clinically appropriate answer from the whole interview, including earlier/later information.

Repurposed Field Logic: The Reliatrax field label does not perfectly match how the clinician uses the field. Follow the clinician’s custom mapping rather than the literal field label.

Default Response Rules
If the client specifies something different than a default, use the client’s reported information.

Default answers should not override clear client report.

If the client directly contradicts a default, the client’s report wins.

n/a Formatting
Use lowercase n/a unless a field-specific instruction says otherwise.

The clinician sometimes referred to “NA” verbally, but the final standardized automation value should be n/a.

Reliatrax Form Splitting
The Reliatrax BPS form is large and may be split into four technical automation parts. This document is one master rule set for the entire form. The four functional sections are:

Living Situation, Substance Use, Tobacco, Withdrawal, Treatment History

Mental Health, Symptoms, Suicide, Self-Harm, Trauma, Grief, Violence

Legal, Family, Spiritual, Cultural, Medical, Medications, Sexual Risk

Vocational, Education, Military, Relationship, Children/CPS, Supports, Hobbies, Gambling/Technology, Strengths/Challenges

Part 1: Living Situation, Substance Use, Tobacco, Withdrawal, and Treatment History
Living Situation
Field: Who referred you to services?
Default:

Enter Self-referral.

Exception:

If the client specifies another referral source, enter the specific referral source instead.

Field: Tell me a bit about why you decided to come to our services.
Type: Direct ask / direct entry.

Rule:

The clinician asks this question directly.

Enter the client’s stated reason for seeking services.

Use a clinically appropriate summary of what the client says.

Field: Where are you currently living?
Type: Direct ask / direct entry.

Rule:

Enter the client’s current living situation.

If client is in sober living, enter the name of the sober living house if provided.

If client is living elsewhere, document the reported setting.

Examples:

Purpose Recovery sober living

Serene sober living

With family

Apartment

Homeless/shelter setting

Field: With whom are you living?
Default for sober living:

Enter Other residents at [name of sober living listed above].

Exception:

If the client reports living with specific people or in a different arrangement, enter what they report.

Substance Use Information
The form includes Substance of Choice 1, 2, and 3. Use the same logic for each substance.

If no second or third substance is reported, enter n/a for that substance section as applicable.

Field: Substance and Age of First Use
Format:

[Substance name]

Age of first use: [age]

Example:

Alcohol

Age of first use: 15

Rule:

Use the substance name and the client’s reported age of first use.

Field: In general, how much were you using?
Default:

Enter Daily use at peak.

Exception:

If client provides a specific amount or pattern, use the client’s specific answer.

Field: How often were you using?
Default:

Enter Daily use at peak.

Exception:

If client provides a specific frequency, use the client’s specific answer.

Field: At any point, did you develop a tolerance?
Default:

Select Yes.

Exception:

If the client specifically states they did not develop tolerance, select No.

Field: Method of Use
Rule:

Enter the client’s reported method of use.

Examples:

oral

smoking

snorting

IV

vaping

inhalation

Field: Date of Last Use
Rule:

Enter the client’s reported last use date.

Use the exact date if provided.

If client gives approximate timing, summarize clearly.

Field: Days Used in the Last 30
Type: Calculated field.

Rule:

Calculate based on the client’s date of last use and reported recent pattern.

If client reports continuous abstinence for more than 30 days, enter 0.

If client reports use within the last 30 days, calculate as accurately as possible from the transcript.

Field: Recent Pattern of Use
Rule:

Enter the client’s reported recent use pattern.

If exact recent pattern is not directly stated, infer from the client’s timeline and reported last use.

Field: Behavioral Changes
Type: Inference field.

Rule:

Infer behavioral changes related to the specific substance based on:

substance-specific information the client reported,

broader interview content,

behavioral consequences described elsewhere,

legal, family, work, mental health, or housing consequences if connected to use.

Use clinically appropriate language.

Field: Consequences
Type: Direct ask / direct entry.

Rule:

Clinician asks about consequences directly.

Enter the consequences the client reports.

Field: Do you see your use of substances as a problem?
Default:

Select Yes.

Exception:

If the client explicitly states they do not see use as a problem, select No.

Field: Have you tried to quit using this substance in the past?
Default:

Select Yes.

Exception:

If the client explicitly states they have not tried to quit, select No.

Field: How long were you sober?
Rule:

Enter the client’s longest reported period of sobriety.

Clinician asks this during the interview.

Field: Are there any other substances you have used in the past?
Rule if no other substances:

Enter [Client first name] reports no other substances used in the past.

Rule if other substances are reported:

List substances and relevant details provided.

Tobacco Assessment
Field: No history of tobacco use
Rule:

Only check this if the client reports no history of tobacco use.

Field: Do you smoke cigarettes, cigars, use chewing tobacco, or vape?
Rule:

Select Yes if the client reports any current tobacco/nicotine use, including yes, current smoking, tobacco, nicotine, or vaping.

Select No if no current tobacco/nicotine use.

If current use is present but the application type is unspecified, treat the application type as both smoking and vaping for the amount/frequency narrative.

Generic current-use statements such as yes, smoke, smoking, smoker, tobacco, or nicotine are not specific application types.

Field: If vape, does it contain nicotine?
Rule:

If client says they vape, assume Yes unless they specify nicotine-free vaping.

If client specifies nicotine-free vaping, select No.

A nicotine-free vape report sets vape_contains_nicotine.no true.

If client does not vape and only uses cigarettes, leave blank.

If client does not use tobacco products, leave blank.

Field: How much do you use and how often?
Rule:

Enter a sentence based on client report.

Do not infer daily frequency when frequency is missing. Document missing frequency as frequency not specified.

Examples:

{Client_Name} reports smoking and vaping, frequency not specified.

{Client_Name} reports smoking and vaping daily.

{Client_Name} reports vaping daily.

{Client_Name} reports smoking cigarettes sometimes.

{Client_Name} reports no tobacco use.

Replace {Client_Name} with the actual client first name. Do not output the literal placeholder.

Field: Do you consider this to be a problem?
If client wants quitting resources:

Select Yes.

If client does not want quitting resources:

Select No.

Field: Are you wanting to quit now?
If client wants quitting resources:

Select Yes.

If client does not want quitting resources:

Select No.

Field: Have you tried to quit in the past?
This field is used to document interest in quitting resources.

If client wants quitting resources:

Enter [Client first name] is interested in resources for quitting.

If client does not want quitting resources:

Enter [Client first name] is not interested in quitting tobacco at this time.

Withdrawal History
Field: No history of withdrawal
Rule:

Check this only if the client reports no withdrawal history.

Field: Have you experienced withdrawal?
Rule:

Select Yes if client reports withdrawal history.

Select No if client reports no withdrawal history.

Field: What symptoms did you experience?
If withdrawal history is present:

List the withdrawal symptoms the client reports.

Clinician asks this directly.

If no withdrawal history:

Enter [Client first name] reports not experiencing any withdrawal symptoms.

Field: Have you had a seizure due to withdrawal?
Rule:

Clinician asks this directly.

If client says yes, select Yes.

If client says no, select No.

If not specified, select No.

Field: How long did your symptoms last?
If withdrawal history is present:

Default to Reports symptoms lasting approximately one week unless the client gives a different duration.

Exception:

If the withdrawal history is for marijuana, default to Reports symptoms lasting approximately 2-3 days unless the client gives a different duration.

If no withdrawal history:

Enter n/a unless the automation requires the field to be left blank. Preferred value is n/a.

Field: How long after your last use did these symptoms begin?
If withdrawal history is present:

Default to Several hours after cessation of use unless the client gives a different onset.

If no withdrawal history:

Enter n/a.

Field: How did you manage withdrawal symptoms?
If withdrawal history is present:

Document how client managed symptoms.

Examples:

detox

hospital

medical care

self-managed

tapering

rest/fluids

If no withdrawal history:

Enter n/a.

Detox and Treatment History
Field: How many times have you detoxed?
Rule:

Enter the number the client reports.

If none, enter 0.

Field: Total number of inpatient/residential treatments
Rule:

Clinician typically asks, “Have you been to rehab?”

If client gives exact inpatient/residential count, enter that.

If client gives a general rehab count without differentiating inpatient vs outpatient, divide the count between inpatient/residential and outpatient unless otherwise specified.

Default split rule:

If client says they have been to rehab 3 times, enter:

inpatient/residential: 2 times

outpatient: 1 time

Use similar reasonable split logic unless client specifies exact categories.

Field: Total number of outpatient treatments
Rule:

Use client’s specific outpatient treatment count if provided.

If only total rehab count is provided, use the split logic above.

Field: Any experience with 12-step programs or other support groups?
Rule:

Select Yes if client reports any 12-step/support group experience.

Select No if client reports none.

Field: Have you ever had a sponsor or mentor?
Rule:

Select Yes if yes.

If not specified, select No.

Field: What was that experience for you?
If no 12-step/support group experience:

Enter [Client first name] reports no experience with 12-step programs or support groups.

If experience exists but details are not provided:

Enter [Client first name] reports participation in [AA/NA/MA/12-step/support group, as applicable].

If client gives details:

Summarize what client reports about participation or experience.

Part 2: Mental Health, Symptoms, Suicide, Self-Harm, Trauma, Grief, and Violence
Mental Health History
Field: No history of mental health
Rule:

Check this only if the client reports never receiving a mental health diagnosis and no relevant mental health history.

If client has ever received a mental health diagnosis, leave unchecked.

Field: Have you ever been given a mental health diagnosis? If yes, state diagnosis.
Rule:

Enter diagnoses the client reports.

Examples:

PTSD

depression

anxiety

bipolar disorder

ADHD

Field: How old were you when you were diagnosed?
Format:

Use a full sentence.

Example:

[Client first name] reports they were diagnosed with PTSD at age 12 and depression at age 13.

Field: Who were you diagnosed by?
Default:

Enter Unknown unless client specifies provider type/name.

Field: Do you agree with the diagnosis?
Rule:

Select Yes or No based on client report.

If client does not directly answer, infer from what they say about the diagnosis.

Field: Were you using any substances at the time of your diagnosis?
Rule:

Select Yes or No based on client report.

If not directly stated, infer based on:

age at diagnosis,

age of first substance use,

substance use timeline.

Field: If yes, do you believe this use impacted your diagnosis?
If client was not using substances at time of diagnosis:

Enter n/a.

If client was using substances at time of diagnosis:

Enter Yes or No based on client report.

If possible, infer from the timeline and reported symptoms.

If client specifically says substance use impacted diagnosis, enter Yes.

If client says it did not, enter No.

Mental Health Treatment
Field: History of psychiatric hospitalizations
Rule:

Clinician asks directly.

Select Yes or No based on report.

Field: Are you working with a psychiatrist?
Rule:

Select Yes if currently working with psychiatrist.

Select No if not currently working with psychiatrist, including when current provider use is not mentioned, regardless of diagnosis, mental health history, or prior treatment.

Field: Are you currently working with an outpatient or intensive outpatient therapist?
Rule:

Select Yes only if currently working with an outpatient or intensive outpatient therapist.

Select No if current therapist use is not mentioned, regardless of diagnosis, mental health history, or prior treatment.

Field: Please provide name and contact information for any mental health professionals you are currently working with.
Rule:

If both current psychiatrist and current therapist are No, enter n/a.

If a current psychiatrist or therapist is reported, include the provider role and therapist and/or psychiatrist name if provided.

Include organization/agency/practice they work for if provided.

Include phone number if provided.

Include additional relevant information such as length of time working together if client reports it.

If a reported current provider detail such as name, organization, or phone number is missing, state that the detail was not provided.

Clinician asks name and organization for psychiatrist and therapist when applicable.

Field: I am not interested in receiving any resources or referrals.
Rule:

Never check this box.

Current Mental Health Symptoms
Field: What current mental health symptoms are you experiencing, if any? Frequency, duration, intensity.
Type: Direct ask / direct entry.

Rule:

Clinician asks directly whether client is experiencing current mental health symptoms.

If yes, list reported symptoms and include frequency/duration/intensity when available.

Include whether symptoms are managed with medication if relevant.

Include whether symptoms are not managed with medication if client reports that.

If no symptoms:

Enter [Client first name] reports they are not experiencing any mental health symptoms currently.

Field: Do you ever see or hear things that other people do not?
Rule:

Clinician asks directly.

Select Yes or No based on client report.

Field: Have you ever received treatment or been in need of treatment for an eating disorder or disordered eating?
Important custom field placement rule:

This field may be used to document hallucination details due to Reliatrax layout.

If client answers Yes to seeing/hearing things other people do not, the expanded details about what they see/hear should be placed in this text box first.

After hallucination details, add a line break and answer the eating disorder question.

If hallucinations are reported:

Format:

[Client first name] reports [details about what they see/hear].

Eating disorder/disordered eating: No.

Eating disorder default:

The clinician does not typically ask this.

If the client has not specifically reported eating disorder/disordered eating history, enter No.

If hallucinations are denied:

Simply enter No for eating disorder/disordered eating unless client specifically reports otherwise.

Suicide Assessment
Field: Are you currently experiencing suicidal thoughts or ideations?
Rule:

Select Yes or No based on client report.

Clinician does not always ask this if client denies current mental health symptoms.

If client denies current mental health symptoms and does not otherwise report current SI, default to No.

If client reports current suicidal thoughts/ideation, select Yes.

Field: Do you have a plan to end your life?
Default:

Select No unless client specifically reports a plan.

Field: Do you have means or a plan to obtain means to enact this plan?
Default:

Select No unless client specifically reports means or plan to obtain means.

Field: Do you have a history of suicidal thoughts or ideations?
Rule:

Select Yes if client reports history of SI.

Select No if client denies history.

Field: What was going on in your life when these thoughts have been especially difficult?
If history of SI is yes:

Clinician asks directly.

Enter what client reports was happening in their life at that time.

If history of SI is no:

Enter n/a.

Field: When did these thoughts occur? Dates and when most recently?
If history of SI is yes:

Clinician asks directly.

Include most recent occurrence.

Format example:

[Client first name] reports the most recent suicidal thoughts occurred [date/timeframe].

If history of SI is no:

Enter n/a.

Field: Can you describe the thoughts you experienced?
If history of SI is yes and client describes thoughts:

Enter the client’s description.

If history of SI is yes but client does not specify details:

Default to a general clinical description such as:

[Client first name] reports experiencing hopelessness and feeling like there were no other options.

If history of SI is no:

Enter n/a.

Field: Do you have a history of suicide attempts?
Rule:

Select Yes or No based on client report.

Field: How many attempts have you had in your lifetime?
If history of attempts is yes:

Enter number of attempts.

Format:

[Client first name] reports [number] lifetime suicide attempt(s).

If no history of attempts:

Enter n/a.

Field: When did this attempt occur? In what ways did you attempt suicide in the past?
If history of attempts is yes:

Enter most recent attempt timing and method.

Format:

[Client first name] reports their most recent attempt occurred [date/timeframe] by [method].

If no attempts:

Enter n/a.

Field: Were you under the influence of alcohol or drugs at the time of attempt?
If history of attempts is yes:

Enter Yes or No if client specifies.

If not specified, enter Unknown.

If no attempts:

Enter n/a.

Field: How do you feel about past attempts now?
If no attempts:

Enter n/a.

If client specifies feelings about past attempts:

Use client’s report.

Default if attempt history exists but current feelings are missing or non-specific:

For exactly one attempt:

Enter {Client_Name} reports regretting their past suicide attempt.

For multiple attempts or unknown count:

Enter {Client_Name} reports regretting past suicide attempts.

Replace {Client_Name} with the actual client first name. Do not output the literal placeholder.

Preserve specific reported feelings when provided.


Field: What are parts of your life that you feel keep you from attempting suicide?
Type: Inference field.

Rule:

Infer protective factors based on the whole interview unless client provides specific protective factors.

Include supports, goals, family, children, faith, recovery, coping skills, sober living, future orientation, treatment engagement, or other hopeful/protective factors.

Field: Is there anything that you feel would push you toward a suicide attempt in the future?
Type: Direct ask / direct entry.

Rule:

Clinician asks directly.

Enter client’s response.

If self-harm history is yes:

[Client first name] reports the most recent self-harm occurred [date/timeframe].

Enter n/a.

If self-harm history is yes:

Enter method reported.

Examples:

cutting

burning

other reported method

If self-harm history is no:

Enter n/a.

Field: How do you feel about past self-harm now?
If self-harm history exists:

Default entry unless otherwise specified:

[Client first name] reports regret regarding past self-harm and does not feel they are at risk of self-harm in the future.

Exception:

If client reports current risk or clinician specifies current risk, do not use the default. Document current risk accurately.

If no self-harm history:

Enter n/a.

Field: Do you have access to means to engage in self-harm?
Default:

Enter n/a.

Field: Is the client at risk for harming themselves?
Type: Inference field.

Rule:

Select Yes or No based on clinical inference from:

current SI,

SI history,

suicide attempts,

self-harm history,

recency,

access to means,

protective factors,

coping mechanisms,

current presentation.

Field: Why or why not?
Rule:

Provide clinical rationale.

Example if no risk:

[Client first name] denies current suicidal ideation, plan, intent, and current self-harm urges. Although they report historical concerns, they identify protective factors and coping mechanisms and do not appear to be at immediate risk of suicide or self-harm at this time.

Example if no history:

[Client first name] denies historical or current suicidal thoughts, suicidal ideation, suicide attempts, or self-harm.

Example if risk present:

Document current risk factors clearly and clinically.

Rule:

Clinician asks directly.

Select Yes or No based on report.

Field: Any current thoughts of hurting others?
Default:

Select No unless otherwise specified.

Field: Have you ever been the perpetrator of domestic abuse or domestic violence?
Rule:

Clinician may ask this directly.

If not asked and client has not reported anything suggesting perpetration of domestic violence, infer No.

If client reports domestic violence perpetration, select Yes.

Field: Is the client at current risk of violence?
Type: Inference field.

Rule:

Select Yes or No based on the whole interview.

Consider:

current thoughts of harming others,

history of violent behavior,

domestic violence history,

whether violence occurred only while intoxicated,

current sobriety,

coping skills,

mental health symptoms,

impulse control,

current environment.

Field: Why or why not?
Rule:

Provide clinical rationale.

Example if no risk:

[Client first name] does not appear to be at current risk of violence, as they deny current thoughts of harming others and do not report current violent behavior.

Example if historical violence but no current risk:

[Client first name] reports historical violent behavior associated with substance use; however, they deny current thoughts of harming others, report current sobriety, and identify coping mechanisms, so they do not appear to be at current risk of violence.

Example if current risk:

Clearly describe current violence risk factors.
